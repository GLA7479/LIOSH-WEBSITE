Grade 6 – Advanced Elementary
Ministry of Education · English Curriculum 2020 (Elementary School)

Focus
- Transition to lower-secondary expectations: independent projects, research tasks and presentations.
- Integration of information literacy (note taking, citing sources, organising data).
- Developing personal voice: opinions, reflections, arguments.

Skills
- Listen to grade-level content (podcasts, science clips) and extract explicit and implicit information.
- Read multi-paragraph informational texts, stories and adapted articles (200–250 words).
- Speak to explain processes, justify opinions, compare ideas and lead short discussions.
- Write structured texts (letters, articles, reports) of 80–120 words with clear organisation.

Grammar & Structures
- Past Continuous, contrast between Past Simple / Past Continuous.
- Future forms (will, going to, present continuous for plans).
- Introductory Present Perfect (have/has + V3) for life experience; zero & first conditionals.
- Extended modal verbs (should, could, might) for advice, possibility, obligation.

Vocabulary Themes
- Global issues (water, energy, sustainability, climate action).
- Culture & identity, biographies, historical figures and national heritage.
- Technology & communication (apps, digital citizenship, media literacy).
- Feelings & relationships, community involvement, entrepreneurship.

Benchmark – End of Grade 6
- Read, analyse and summarise grade-level texts citing key evidence.
- Conduct short research (collect information, organise it, present findings orally/in writing).
- Produce multi-paragraph writing with accurate tense control and cohesive devices.
- Participate in discussions, pose clarifying questions and respond with elaborated answers.
