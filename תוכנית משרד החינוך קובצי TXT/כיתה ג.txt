Grade 3 – Beginning Literacy
Ministry of Education · English Curriculum 2020 (Elementary School)

Focus
- Full integration of the four skills with systematic reading comprehension.
- Independent decoding and spelling of high-frequency words.
- Building short interactions (questions/answers, mini-dialogues).

Skills
- Listen to short dialogues/instructions with two to three steps.
- Read short narratives/descriptions (60–80 words) and identify main ideas.
- Speak in short interactions: introduce self, describe routines, express likes.
- Write simple sentences (subject + verb + complement) with correct capitalisation/punctuation.

Grammar & Structures
- Present Simple (affirmative, negative, questions), basic adverbs of frequency.
- Adjectives (size, color, personality), comparative “bigger than” (informal).
- Prepositions of place (in, on, under, next to) and articles (a/an/the).

Vocabulary Themes
- Daily routine, school life, hobbies & sports, weather & seasons.
- Body parts, extended animals, basic technology words (computer, phone).
- Days/months, celebrations, basic adjectives (long, short, tall, fast, slow).

Benchmark – End of Grade 3
- Read a short text and answer literal questions in English.
- Produce 4–5 spoken/written sentences to describe people, places or routines.
- Use Present Simple accurately with subject–verb agreement for he/she/it.
- Apply basic reading strategies (preview, picture clues) and show comprehension.
