Grade 4 – Developing Literacy
Ministry of Education · English Curriculum 2020 (Elementary School)

Focus
- Extend reading/writing length (short paragraphs, simple informational texts).
- Encourage independent use of strategies: predicting, skimming, scanning.
- Strengthen speaking for description, opinion and comparison.

Skills
- Read texts of 100–150 words (stories, fact files, emails) and extract details.
- Listen to multi-step instructions or short factual talks and note key information.
- Speak to describe pictures, express preferences and give reasons (“because …”).
- Write short paragraphs (3–5 sentences) with cohesive devices (and, but, because).

Grammar & Structures
- Present Simple (full range) and Present Continuous for actions now.
- Countable vs. uncountable nouns, some/any, much/many.
- Possessive adjectives and pronouns, adverbs of manner (quickly, carefully).
- Comparative & superlative adjectives within the taught lexical sets.

Vocabulary Themes
- Food groups & healthy habits, places in town, transportation.
- Festivals, traditions, cultural celebrations, emotions & character traits.
- Ecosystems, animals & habitats, environmental actions (recycle, save water).

Benchmark – End of Grade 4
- Compose a coherent paragraph describing an event, place or routine (40–60 words).
- Understand instructions or short talks containing two to three details.
- Use Present Continuous alongside Present Simple with few errors.
- Apply vocabulary from thematic units when speaking/writing about daily life.
