Grade 5 – Extended Literacy
Ministry of Education · English Curriculum 2020 (Elementary School)

Focus
- Strategic reading of informational texts (headings, captions, graphic organisers).
- Structured writing: topic sentence, supporting details, closing idea.
- Oral presentations that explain, compare and persuade.

Skills
- Listen to short informational clips/interviews and identify main facts.
- Read articles, brochures and biographies (150–200 words) and infer meaning from context.
- Speak in longer turns (8–10 sentences) to describe processes, give instructions or retell events.
- Write multi-paragraph texts (60–80 words) with cohesive devices (first, next, finally).

Grammar & Structures
- Past Simple (regular and frequent irregular verbs), present vs. past contrasts.
- Future with “will” and “be going to”, modal verbs (can, must, have to).
- Comparatives & superlatives with multi-syllable adjectives, quantifiers (few, a little).
- Introduction to complex sentences with when/because/if (zero conditional).

Vocabulary Themes
- Travel & transportation, geography, global citizenship.
- Health & the human body, nutrition, fitness, safety rules.
- Environment & nature (recycle, pollution, energy, wildlife).
- Technology basics (devices, internet safety), media and communication.

Benchmark – End of Grade 5
- Summarise the main idea and two supporting details from a grade-level text.
- Deliver a short spoken presentation using visuals or cue cards.
- Write two connected paragraphs using correct tense and punctuation.
- Apply past/future verb forms and modal verbs accurately in familiar contexts.
